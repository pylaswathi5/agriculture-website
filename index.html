Introduction

The practice of cultivating the soil, producing crops, and keeping livestock is referred to as farming. Agriculture is critical to a country’s economic development.Nearly 58% of a country’s primary source of livelihood is farming.Most of the population in the rural areas is dependent on agriculture as their main source of income.Agriculture is the backbone of many economies, providing food, raw materials, and employment.

The main objective of agriculture is to produce food for the people, though several things affect the agricultural sectors. The cultivation of plant and food grains began years ago in order to provide food to the city population. Agriculture is the main need for the people to live in the society. Agriculture is the main source of livelihood, it provides a source for the people to earn. The major agricultural products can be widely grouped into categories of food grains, fibers and raw materials.

Climate Change

Problem:

Climate change leads to irregular rainfall, droughts, and extreme weather conditions, impacting crop production.

Solution:

Adopt climate-smart agriculture practices, such as drought-resistant crops and improved irrigation techniques.

Water Scarcity

Problem:

Agricultural activities consume a significant portion of freshwater resources, leading to shortages.

Solution:

Implement efficient water management systems, like drip irrigation, pipelines and rainwater harvesting.

Soil Degradation

Problem:

Overuse of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, along with poor farming practices, and cultivating same crop leads to soil erosion and loss of fertility.

Solution:

Implementing rotational grazing and proper waste management systems for livestock can improve soil fertility and reduce environmental pollution.

Pest and Diseases

Problem:

Pests and diseases can devastate crops, leading to significant losses.

Solution:

Use integrated pest management and genetically modified crops for better resistance.

Market Volatility

Problem:

Farmers face income instability due to fluctuating market prices for their produce.

Solution:

Develop and implement policies that stabilize prices and provide financial support to farmers

Technological Gaps

Problem:

Limited access to modern agricultural technology restricts productivity

Solution:

Facilitate access to advanced technology like drone technology, pipe line systems, solar system through subsidies and training programs.

Conventional Farming

Problem:

Over usage of synthetic chemical fertilizers, pesticides, herbicides. These chemicals are particularly dangerous in fruits and vegetables by which people are exposed to them.

Solution:

Organic agriculture is a production system that regenerates the health of soils, ecosystems, and people. Organic farmers rely on natural processes rather than the use of synthetic inputs like chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides.

Weeds in Farming

Problem:

Weeds are unwanted plants that grow among crops. They compete with crops for sunlight, water, nutrients, and space, which can lead to lower crop yields and poorer crop quality.

Solution:

Deep ploughing is a farming technique where the soil is turned over to a deeper level than usual (more than 20-30 cm). This helps control weeds by burying their seeds and disrupting their growth.

subsidies and schemes

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana (PM-Kisan Yojana) is a government scheme through which, all small and marginal farmers will get up to Rs 6,000 per year as minimum income support.

PM Kisan Samman Nidhi

The Soil Health Card (SHC) scheme is a government of India program that provides farmers with a printed report on the health of their soil. The SHC includes information on the soil's nutrient status, as well as recommendations for fertilizers and soil amendments.

Soil Health Card

The Sub Mission on Seed and Planting Material (SMSP) seeks to ensure the production and multiplication of high yielding, certified/quality seeds of all crops so that farmers may get enough seeds at a reasonable cost and in a timely manner.

Sub Mission on Seed and Planting Material

The Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM) is a program by the Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Government of India that aims to increase the use of farm machinery by small and marginal farmers.

Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization

The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) is to improve water use efficiency in agriculture, promotes the use of precision irrigation and other water-saving technologies, such as drip and sprinkler irrigation. The government provides financial assistance to farmers for installing micro irrigation systems. The assistance is 55% for small and marginal farmers, and 45% for other farmers.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana
